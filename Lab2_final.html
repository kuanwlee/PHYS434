From the exercises in last lab and this lab, we averaged non-Gaussian distributions over many intervals and observed how the distribution evolved. For a distribution like Rayleigh and Poisson, the distribution approaches to a Gaussian distribution. The shape of the distribution stopped changing very much after averaging certain amount of times. This implies that the senitivity penalty stop increasing at some point. To make sense of this, we just need to realize that the non-Gaussian distribution approaches to a Gaussian after averaged over many times, and the sigma of that Gaussian distribution approximation does not keep changing. Instead, it converges to a certain value. In summary, a non-Gaussian distribution's sensitivity penalty does not keep increasing but the sensitivity penalty of a Gaussian distribution will become infinite if the distribution is averaged over too many times.
